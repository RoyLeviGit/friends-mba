sections:
  - heading: Who We Are
    paragraphs:
      - "The IDF Medical Battalion stands at the most sensitive and critical junctures of the State of Israel. Our mission is to provide life-saving medical response, with nationwide deployment and the ability to adapt rapidly and precisely to any operational and national need."
      - 'Throughout the years, and in the face of every challenge the state has encountered, our people have been there on the front lines. From the COVID-19 crisis, through October 7th, throughout the entire Iron Swords war, and through complex operations such as "Im Kilavi" and "Shaagat HaAri". The battalion''s professionalism is measured in real time, as we operate shoulder to shoulder with the Air Force and support combat forces in every sector, or when we partner in the most highly classified missions with Israel''s special mission units. Our teams are alert and ready to deploy at any given moment, in routine and in emergency.'
  - heading: Strategic Medical Umbrella
    paragraphs:
      - "The responsibility placed on the battalion's shoulders is expressed in a wide range of national missions: we were there in the most moving and sacred moments, on the ambulances that received the hostages returning home at the helipads."
      - "Within the Medical Brigade, the Blood Services battalion operates alongside us, providing critical response in mass-casualty events. We work in close cooperation with investigation and identification units, and operate a dedicated surgical unit prepared to establish a field hospital the moment a rear hospital is hit."
  - heading: The Human Mosaic — Our True Strength
    paragraphs:
      - "Beyond missions and technology, the battalion's true power lies in its people. The battalion is a microcosm of Israeli society at its best: men and women serve together, shoulder to shoulder, Jews and Druze, members of the LGBTQ+ community, and even couples who share the reserve duty bench."
      - "This is an organic family where teammates also work together in civilian life, celebrate together at happy occasions, and continue to volunteer for active reserve service long after the exemption age. We are especially proud to serve as a leading model in the IDF for women's empowerment, with approximately 50% women serving in the battalion, a female deputy battalion commander, and an impressive female majority among our company commanders."
  - heading: The Association's Vision and Goals
    paragraphs:
      - "The Friends of the IDF Medical Battalion Association was founded with the goal of standing behind the people who stand on the front lines. The association's vision is guided by three overarching goals: action for Israeli society, continuous care for battalion veterans and active members, and assistance with professional and career development."
      - "Many of our people, who began their journey as young medics graduating from Training Base 10, fell in love with the world of medicine and over the years became senior physicians and specialists in the healthcare system. The most prominent example of this excellence is a battalion alumnus, Nobel Prize laureate in Chemistry, Prof. Aaron Ciechanover. To continue nurturing this excellence, the association actively works to raise and distribute scholarships for students from among our members."
      - "Alongside professional development, the association works tirelessly to strengthen the resilience of our human network: improving morale through events for reservists, creating essential moments of respite for fighters and caregivers on the front, and providing support and a warm embrace to families in the home front who share the burden with us."
      - "We invite you to be partners in this endeavor, to provide a tailwind for our work, and to join this special family. Together, we will continue to save lives and build a stronger, healthier, and more united Israeli society."
  - heading: Our History
    paragraphs:
      - "The battalion was founded as a unique unit from day one — with a sense of mission, professionalism, and unit pride built from nothing. Over decades, the internal bond turned the battalion into a true family, with a loyalty that extends far beyond the years of service. From Yom Kippur 1973 to the Iron Swords war, the battalion's people have been there at every decisive moment."
