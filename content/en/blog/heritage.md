A unique unit from day one. Maturity, accountability. Someone you can count on.

## The Founder and the Vision

There is no doubt that all of us, carefully selected by the battalion's founder, were instilled with a sense of uniqueness, professionalism, and the standard of conduct required. In every location, whenever needed, while maintaining the rules that were dictated, enforced, and re-examined each time.

The founder, who arrived in an antenna-laden "Carmel-Duke" vehicle packed with advanced and impressive equipment, inspected every detail in the fullest sense of the word — every tag on a uniform, every piece of gear, every act of compliance in individual and group performance. And the truth must be said: he created unit pride from nothing. Only those with the right clearance knew its secrets, capabilities, and operations.

## The Regular Class

In early 1972, the regular class was established by the battalion's founder. Initially there were only 6 members, and later another 6 joined. Beyond the regular activities of the battalion, this class was designated to provide medical response for missions requiring high security classification.

## The Bond That Became Family

The unique internal cohesion was a dominant factor in the joy of reserve service. The professional uniqueness of every team member across all roles and skills — and it was no coincidence that our company commander was a psychiatrist. The deputy company commander ensured the link between skills and performance, while maintaining location secrecy even during leave. We had a dedicated mechanic and a dedicated chef. We never gave up on either of them.

More than once, our wives and children wondered what joy seized us when we were called up for reserves. We tried to explain, not always successfully, but the shared service was beneficial and efficient in every aspect. Its effects on the friendships between many of us lasted well beyond age 50+, when we were reluctantly discharged from reserve duty. Everyone looked after everyone — from every shoelace to engine oil, from shoe size to the quality of the elaborate field stew.

## Loyalty, Not Medals

We have no legacy of valor medals and decorations — only genuine loyalty to our mission here in the Land of Israel. This loyalty is an asset earned through long years of service and unique pride, in unique roles.

It would be fitting if they considered drafting us again — provided we don't have to endure the nightmare of quartermaster inventories and equipment counts.

Our blessings to those who carry the torch.
