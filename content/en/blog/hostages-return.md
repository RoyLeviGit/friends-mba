Friday, October 10, 2025. Messages in the group chats start flying in the morning hours. 'Immediate standby, who can make it?' Usually, mobilizing a reserve force on a Friday morning is a logistical challenge, but this time something different happened.

Within minutes, the lists filled up. People left their Shabbat preparations, their families, and their plans, and put on uniforms. Motivation was at its peak — everyone understood we were facing a historic moment. There were no questions of 'why,' only requests to join. We reached a point where we had to tell people 'no room,' because all positions were filled within moments.

## The Tense Wait

The force split into two main hubs: the Soroka Hospital helipad and the Beilinson Hospital helipad. The mission: to receive 5 hostages returning home and provide them with initial medical support if needed.

Shabbat came in, and we were there. Maximum alertness. Hours turned into days, and the tension was palpable.

## Monday: The Moment We Waited For

Only on Monday, October 13 (holiday eve), it happened. The sound of helicopters pierced the silence. When the helicopter landed at Beilinson and the doors opened, 5 returnees stepped out.

For us, the doctors and fighters standing there at the helipad, it was a moment of catharsis. After an entire weekend of uncertainty and waiting, seeing them step off the helicopter onto Israeli soil was worth every sacrifice.
