In the battalion, everyone knows the commander as the one who leads us with composure in exercises and in war. We're used to seeing him in a vest, helmet, and radio, managing complex events under pressure. But when he takes off the uniform, our battalion commander manages battles of a completely different kind.

In civilian life, the commander runs a leading boutique law firm with a unique and fascinating specialization.

## Between Israel and Europe

His office is no ordinary law firm. With over 20 years of experience, he specializes in two main areas: complex real estate in Israel, and a unique niche of **German law and German citizenship**.
His command of the German language and his international connections have made him a central address for Israelis seeking to exercise rights in Germany or conduct real estate transactions in Europe.

## 'Values Are Values'

When asked about the connection between commanding a medical battalion and practicing law, his answer is simple: 'In the end, it all starts and ends with people and responsibility. Just as in the battalion I'm responsible for human lives and executing the mission as precisely as possible, so too in the office — the client entrusts me with their assets and future. There's no room for mistakes.'

This combination, between the warrior who runs in the field and the polished attorney who represents companies and corporations, is what makes the human capital of the Friends of the Medical Battalion Association so special.
